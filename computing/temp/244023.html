Dealing with Formulas and Formatting On Excel

In this article, will show you how to format your orders and calculate them apart. Let's consider we would have two orders and we want to make them look better by formating them and adjusting them.

Will be instructing you how to format your cells appropriately for the values representation and how we will combine those together to update our orders.

This will be our default spreadsheet:

Default structure of the spreadsheet.

The Structure:

The Sheets
  • Order1 as orders from sheet 1
  • Order2 as orders from sheet 2
  • OrderUpdate as combined orders from both sheets

Order1 would feature 3 items ordered another order placed would feature an additonal of 2 items ordered for Order2. The combined orders will be added together inside OrderUpdate of all items ordered.

Formatting Our Spreadsheet

We will turn this format into a format we want to see our data being represented with. Basically well only worry about our currency values which are the costs calls.

By general Excel uses the current format as "General" which means anything can be used in the cell just means you can manually enter what you want.

If want your cells to be currency, you can enter £ followed by a number but this is alot of hassle. You'll want to change your cell format to "Currency" that are supposed to be your currency cells by default.

Formatting the spreadsheet to currency format.

In the example below will show you how to change your cell format to Currency-based format.

The Sheets
  1. Select your first cell Example "B3"
  2. Drag to last cell Example "B6"
  3. Right click and select "Format Cells"
  4. You'll see the current format is "General"
  5. On the list select "Currency"
  6. Choose the decimal you require by default it's "2"
  7. If you want to remove it, enter "0" as the value
  8. By default you'll see the symbol as "£"
  9. Click "OK" with your preferred settings

You should see the example picture as currency values.

The spreadsheet is updated as currency format.

Calculating Orders and Costs

Anything can be possible for Excel, you can work with different sheets to perform tasks you require done with your tables. Individual sheets seperates your work into chunks.

Especially when it comes to shopping or producing orders, different sheets can be referred to having daily, monthly and annual concepts.

For example one sheet could be January Orders another can be Feburary Orders you wouldn't want orders of every month! so you could have a count of orders from each specific month.

Sometimes it can complicated to structure the formula of your totals depending on whether its the same row or different rows.

In this example will show you two orders "Order1" and "Order2" sheets and a total sheet for counting my order of items and costs inside "OrderUpdate" from different sheets.

Two ways to make this less complicated based on the formulas:

Same Row Formula Concept:

My Order1 sheet would have Totals in Cell D6, my Order2 sheet would have Totals in D6 also so this just means they are in the same row! Easy! I can just use the formula as =SUM(Sheet:Sheet!CellReference).

The formula you'd use would be =SUM(Order1:Order2!D6) as shown below.

Same row calculation.

Different Row Formula Concept:

If my Order1 sheet would have item totals in cell C6 and my Order2 sheet has item totals in the cell C6 they are in different rows. I'd have to use a seperator , in the formula which would be =SUM(Sheet!CellReference,Sheet!CellReference).

The formula you'd use would be =SUM(Order1!D6,Order2!D5) as shown below.
